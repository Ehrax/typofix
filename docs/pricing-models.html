typofix — Pricing Model Decision

Pricing and channel analysis for typofix, a macOS menu-bar utility: press a hotkey, the selected text anywhere on screen gets its typos fixed by an LLM through a capped server-side proxy.

Internal decision doc · July 2026 · Assumes Haiku-class model, AI cost ≈ $0.05–$0.30 per active user per month, hard-capped server-side.

Key takeaway

Fixed per-transaction fees dominate at micro prices. At $0.99/mo, Stripe's $0.30 floor eats ~33% while MAS takes 15% ($0.15) — MAS wins monthly. At $9.99/yr, Stripe drops to 5.9% and beats both MAS (15%) and Paddle/LS (10%) — Stripe wins annual. Whatever the channel, push annual.

Honest framing

At $0.99–$9.99 price points, typofix is side-project scale until several thousand paying subscribers. 100 subs nets ~$70/mo. The economics only work because AI cost is hard-capped and marginal cost per user stays under ~$0.30/mo — protect that cap.

5Recommendation

Recommended path

  • First, verify sandboxing: confirm the Accessibility-based select-and-fix flow survives App Store sandbox rules. This is the gating question.
  • If MAS works: launch on the Mac App Store (Small Business Program, 15%) — cheapest micro-billing, zero tax work, built-in trust and discovery for an unknown utility.
  • If sandbox blocks it: distribute direct with Stripe, annual-first ($9.99/yr front and center; hide or drop the $0.99 monthly, where Stripe's $0.30 fixed fee costs ~33%). Use Stripe Tax; budget time for accounts/licensing, or use Paddle/LS if you'd rather trade ~4 points of fee for zero tax/account work.
  • Push annual everywhere — lowest fee drag, lowest churn, best cash flow.
  • Keep the free tier tight (~20 fixes/day, hard server cap) — it is the funnel, not the product.
  • Offer BYO-key ($4.99) as a power-user escape hatch; avoid lifetime unless it carries a hard lifetime token budget.
All figures are estimates for planning purposes. Fees, AI pricing, and store terms change — re-verify before launch.
